About

I'm a student at Vanderbilt University pursuing degrees in both mathematics and computer science. I love the excitement of working with new technologies and smashing the most difficult to find bugs. My primary choice of area of development is iOS and Python scripting. I'm familiar with the selenium webdriver in Python and have worked with most of the UIKit in Swift.

Projects

Greenville Parking – my team won the Imagine Upstate Transportation Hackathon by producing an application that allows parking lots in downtown Greenville to keep track of the number of available spots in their lot and facilitates reservation of empty lots. The parking attendant keeps track of the number of spots open by using the intuitive interface provided, pushing the number of open lots to a server to be displayed to users. If a lot has open spaces, users can reserve spots.

DHG Scripts – I wrote some Python scripts using Selenium while interning at Dixon Hughes Goodman. The scripts were used for automating tasks, including the gathering of hundreds of urgent care location that were copied and pasted one at a time beforehand.

Trolley Tracker (Code for Greenville) – tells the user where the trolley in downtown Greenville currently is and when it will arrive at its next stop. Worked primarily on the feedback services. Written in Swift.
